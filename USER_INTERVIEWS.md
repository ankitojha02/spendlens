# USER_INTERVIEWS.md

## Interview 1 — R.K.

**Role:** Indie Hacker Founder  
**Company Stage:** Bootstrapped SaaS (4 employees)

### Direct Quotes

- "We started using AI tools randomly and now nobody knows the total monthly spend."
- "ChatGPT, Claude, and Cursor overlap a lot for us."
- "The API bill is harder to predict than SaaS subscriptions."

### Most Surprising Insight

The founder cared more about predictable billing than maximum savings.

### What It Changed In The Product

I made the audit output emphasize annual waste and clearer optimization recommendations instead of only showing monthly savings.

## Interview 2 — A.S.

**Role:** Engineering Manager  
**Company Stage:** Seed-stage startup (12 employees)

### Direct Quotes

- "We bought team plans too early because we assumed we'd scale fast."
- "Some engineers barely use the licenses assigned to them."
- "Nobody reviews AI tooling costs during engineering planning."

### Most Surprising Insight

The company had no process for auditing unused AI seats even though tooling costs were increasing monthly.

### What It Changed In The Product

I added logic for identifying small teams overpaying for enterprise or team plans and added recommendations around plan downgrades.

## Interview 3 — P.M.

**Role:** Student Founder  
**Company Stage:** Early prototype startup

### Direct Quotes

- "I use whatever AI tool Twitter recommends that week."
- "I didn’t realize I was paying for overlapping AI subscriptions."
- "I would definitely use a free tool that explains where I'm overspending."

### Most Surprising Insight

The founder had never compared AI vendors side-by-side despite spending every month on multiple subscriptions.

### What It Changed In The Product

I simplified the audit experience and focused on making the savings summary easy to screenshot and share publicly.
